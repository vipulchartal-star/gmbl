# GMBL Pitch Deck

## 1. Cover
GMBL  
Swipe-first social betting for the next generation of players

Tagline: One outcome per screen. Bet in seconds.

## 2. The Problem
Sports betting apps are built like spreadsheets.

Players face:
- cluttered market screens
- slow bet selection flows
- confusing back/lay mechanics
- weak product identity for younger mobile-native users

Most products were designed for desktop-era habits, not short-session mobile behavior.

## 3. The Insight
A large share of users now discover, compare, and act through feed-based interfaces.

What works on modern mobile:
- one focused decision at a time
- immediate context
- strong visual feedback
- low-friction action loops

Betting UX has not fully adapted to this behavior shift.

## 4. The Solution
GMBL turns betting into a swipeable feed.

Core experience:
- one bet card per screen
- clear Back and Lay actions on the same card
- live price and return visibility
- instant bet placement
- looping feed after each bet
- top-bar slips and wallet visibility

This makes betting faster, clearer, and more engaging.

## 5. Product Demo
Current product flow:
- create account with login id and password
- swipe through markets like Toss, Bookmaker, Match Odds
- tap Back or Lay directly from the card
- see returns before betting
- receive visual success feedback on placement
- view placed bets from the slips menu

## 6. Why We Win
GMBL is not just another sportsbook skin.

Differentiators:
- feed-native betting UX
- simplified outcome-first decision model
- explicit education for back/lay betting
- high-speed one-handed mobile flow
- designed for repeat action, not menu browsing

## 7. Market Opportunity
Global sports betting is large and still growing, but interface innovation is weak.

Opportunity areas:
- younger first-time bettors
- high-frequency casual players
- emerging social betting formats
- creators and communities that want shareable betting experiences

We win by improving product experience, not just offering more markets.

## 8. Business Model
Near-term monetization:
- margin on pricing
- market-making spread
- tournament or event promotions
- VIP and retention incentives

Longer-term expansion:
- creator-led markets
- sponsored bet feeds
- community competitions
- premium analytics and engagement tools

## 9. Go-To-Market
Launch wedge:
- cricket-first audience with high mobile engagement
- focus on marquee matches and simple high-intent markets
- short-form creator distribution
- referral loops around bet slips and social sharing

Growth loops:
- swipe, bet, share
- return to app for settlement and next market
- repeat via event-driven engagement
